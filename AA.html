Adventurer's Assistant

Using technology to make Dungeons and Dragons more accessible to new players.

Dungeons and Dragons has a significant barrier to entry. To get started, you need to read the Player’s Handbook, a comprehensive guide that covers character classes, rules of combat, magic spells, and more. Many new players are intimidated by this and lose interest.

Research

COMPETITOR FINDINGS

  • Too much information on similar apps, hard to tell where to start.

  • They target experienced players, new players still face a steep learning curve.

CARD SORT

To simplify the experience on a small screen size, I wanted to know how players might group information from the character sheet. I used this to help users navigate between important information.

Wireframing

Simple Character Creation

The most important step to getting started. To me, “simple” meant able to complete without referencing outside materials.

In-game Updates

This could be adding new items, learning a new spell, or changing their stats.

Customization

D&D fans are creative. I wanted my design to allow customization to spells, items, classes, or anything else.

Usability Testing

USER FEEDBACK

  • New users liked the introduction to each class, but felt lost in later steps.

  • Some screens were overwhelming to new users, certain game information wasn't explained.

  • Experienced users had quick access to all the information they wanted.

UPDATED DESIGN GOALS

  • Simplify screens to help new users find and understand information.

  • Guide users through the character creation process.

Next Steps
